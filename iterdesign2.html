UI/UX: Iterative Design of Emerge Career

About Emerge Career

Emerge career seeks to bridge the gap between employment and individuals post-incarceration. Emerge does so by working with governments to provide online job training in industries that are labor-starved. Additionally, by providing this type of training, Emerge helps to reduce the rate at which previously incarcerated individuals reoffend and thus reduces the rate of overall crime.

Users impacted by Emerge Career are those who are re-entering society after being incarcerated. Every year, there are about 600,000 individuals being released from state and federal prisons, and another 9 million from local prisons.

Exploring Design Alternatives

Design Concept #1
    Unique design choices:
  • Landing page assumes user is returning user
  • Navbar is horizontally displayed at the top
  • Content is sectioned off by borders
  • Two column layout
  • Buttons have borders
Design Concept #2
    Unique design choices:
  • Landing page has minimal information
  • Landing page assumes user is new user
  • Two different navbars with opposite alignments
  • Vertical navbar is right aligned
  • Vertical navbar shows page specific content: current lesson vs quiz question
Design Concept #3
    Unique design choices:
  • Landing page has less visuals/images than other designs
  • Landing page assumes user new but presents much more information
  • Small image accompanies all course and job descriptions
  • Users have the option to filter content
  • Users have the option to save courses and jobs of interest
Design Concept #4
    Unique design choices:
  • Landing pages presents both extensive information and visuals for new users
  • Single navbar with left aligned, centered, and right aligned content
  • Navbar is collapsed
  • Buttons are rounded
  • Content is sectioned off using horizontal breaks

High Fidelity Mockup

Based on our wireframes, we created a high-fidelity protoype of the application to be critiqued by our peers.

Critique Feedback

The problems peers found with our prototype include:

  1. The text, specifically titles, of the Training page should be clickable
  2. Capitalize "u" in "Contact us"
  3. Highlight sidebar to indicate what section you are currently on
  4. The progress bar should include text for how much you've completed on the training page
  5. The Emerge Career title and About sections of the nav bar redundantly navigate to the same page
  6. The green of the navbar is too dark - creates low contrast
  7. Filler words ("lorem ipsum..") should not be included in the final prototype
  8. Font hierarchy of Training page. Specifically, course titles should have lower hierarchy

The changes we made to specifically address each problem include:

  1. Made the titles of Training page clickable
  2. Capitalized "Contact Us"
  3. The section which you are on is bolded on the navbar
  4. The Training page progress bar lists how many sections have been completed out of total sections
  5. The Emerge Career title no longer navigates to the same page as About on the navbar (it is just a title)
  6. Made the navbar color lighter to increase contrast
  7. Replaced all filler words with content you would expect to see in the real application
  8. Courses titles now have lower hierarchy that "Your Courses"

User Testing

To further test our design, we were provided three free user tests on usertesting.com by Brown.

In these tests, the users attempted to navigate our website to sign in and view the latest lesson in a specified course.

Instructions

"This is a prototype of a website that provides online education and job search opportunities for formerly incarcerated individuals. Imagine that you are a student who is logging onto the website. You are currently enrolled in a course that will certify you to operate heavy machinery. Navigate to the course and start the next section to continue your training. We encourage you to think aloud as you navigate the prototype. Note that this is a prototype and not a finished website, so some functionality may not work."

Tasks

  1. Without leaving the homepage, what are your initial impressions of the website? Explain your answer.
  2. Sign in to the website and navigate to the "Training" page. [Success: Yes, No] [5-point rating scale: Very difficult to Very easy]
  3. Navigate to the "Heavy Machinery Certification" course and begin the next section, "Operating Procedures". [Success: Yes, No] [5-point rating scale: Very difficult to Very easy]
  4. How intuitive was it to navigate our prototype and reach the desired section? [5-point Rating scale: Very difficult to Very easy]

Analysis of Results

  • People generally found our prototype to be understandable and clear, and all three were able to complete our tasks.
  • A few users had issues with figma, such as the demo being too large for the screen or the figma bar hiding certain features.
  • Some users had a bit of trouble with the sign in flow, particularly about whether they needed to create an account or just click the sign in button, but that seemed to be mostly a function of Figma and our task descriptions being unclear.
  • Some users mentioned that finding the correct section within the course was a bit difficult

Takeaways

Designing iteratively helped us improve upon each design in different stages:

  • Sketching is an easy medium to get a feel for the website.
  • Wireframing helps to clean up a sketch and view the layout more easily.
  • The first mockup is essentially a high fidelity version of the wireframe, with content and colors added. User feedback on this version is especially helpful, as this design is close to the final version.
  • The second mockup is the culmination of the previous steps, producing a website that has been adjusted according to feedback by multiple users.
  • Submitting our final version to a user testing website provides us feedback that we can use to analyze our design and see potential interface improvements.
